Gemini Pro 3 successfully proved termination (`FruitProof.lean`) by abandoning the implicit assumption that the measure must be additive. instead, it utilized a **Polynomial Interpretation** with two key properties:

1.  **Multiplicative Recursion**: The measure of the recursive operator (`banana`/`recΔ`) is defined as a product of its arguments:
    ```lean
    measure (banana b s n) = measure b + measure s * measure n
    ```
    This makes `s` a *coefficient* for the recursive argument `n`.

2.  **Weighted Constructors**:
    *   `grape` (`delta`) adds **2** to the measure: `measure (grape t) = measure t + 2`.
    *   `pear` (`app`) adds **1** to the measure: `measure (pear t1 t2) = t1 + t2 + 1`.

### The Mathematics of the Solution

Let's analyze the inequality for `R_apple_orange` (`rec_succ`) using these definitions:

**LHS**: `banana b s (grape n)`
$$ M_{LHS} = M(b) + M(s) \cdot M(grape(n)) $$
$$ M_{LHS} = M(b) + M(s) \cdot (M(n) + 2) $$
$$ M_{LHS} = M(b) + M(s) \cdot M(n) + 2 \cdot M(s) $$

**RHS**: `pear s (banana b s n)`
$$ M_{RHS} = M(s) + M(banana(b, s, n)) + 1 $$
$$ M_{RHS} = M(s) + (M(b) + M(s) \cdot M(n)) + 1 $$

**The Decrease Condition**: $M_{LHS} > M_{RHS}$
$$ M(b) + M(s)M(n) + 2M(s) > M(b) + M(s)M(n) + M(s) + 1 $$

Subtracting common terms ($M(b) + M(s)M(n)$):
$$ 2M(s) > M(s) + 1 $$

Subtracting $M(s)$:
$$ M(s) > 1 $$

Since the base measure (`plum`) is define as 2, and all measures are strictly positive sums of naturals, $M(s) \ge 2$.
Therefore, **$M(s) > 1$ always holds.**
